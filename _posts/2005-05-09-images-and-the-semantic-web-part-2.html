I've been doing a fair bit of research into the association of
information with other information (metadata) and in particular the
association of some semantic information with digital image data. While
I was using adobe photo albumn to organize my personal photos I
associated a lot of data (captions etc) with my images. Unfortunately
this didn't translate well when I decided that Picasa was the bomb and
that I'd rather use that instead thank you so very much.

Hence my fascination with the use of EXIF data to store information
about my images within the images themselves. The EXIF data associated
with an image is normally used (by casual digital photographers at
least) to store information generated by the camera. this can include
such information as whether the flash fired, the amount of white
balance, the distance from the camera of the main subject, which
direction is up (if your camera has a gravity sensor). It can also be
used to store descriptions of the image itself.

This however tends to be more the domain of the IPTC embedded data. The
IPTC tags are a way for (professional) photographers to associate
descriptions, captions, authorship and copyright notices with their
images in digital format (in a manner which can't be easily seperated
from the image itself). Actually this is misleading, it is easy to
strip this information from an image if you are thus inclined but by
being in the same file inadvertent loss is reduced.

This standard is currently being superceded by the Extensible Metadata Platform (XMP)
standard. I say standard but it is actually in main the work of one
company (Adobe) which brought this platform to life. The XMP is based
upon the Rich Data Format (RDF - sick of my use of abbreviations yet?)
and is meant as a consistent crossplatform framework for storing
metadata about an image.
